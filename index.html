Fragrance

Fragrance is defined by the FDA as a combination of chemicals that gives each perfume or cologne (including those used in other products) its distinct scent. Fragrance ingredients may be derived from petroleum or natural raw materials.

The words fragrance and scent are usually used synonymously, the difference is fine: while fragrance denotes a pleasant fragrance, scent is used for an unmistakable fragrance. Both refer to pleasant smells: while aroma refers more to plants, spices and food, fragrance is more associated with perfume and flowers.

A fragrance is a smell, usually a pleasant or sweet smell. Often perfumes are called fragrances. If you like the fragrance of coffee, you like its smell. Unlike the word odor, which usually means a bad smell, fragrances are usually good smells.

Odors in the fragrant category included lavender, soap and cologne, while freshly cut grass and mushrooms gave off a woody/resinous smell. Eucalyptus, camphor and tea leaves were considered minty/peppermint scents. Sweet odors included vanilla, almond and chocolate. Kerosene and ammonia fell into the chemical category.20 sept. 2013

Fruity

Fruity perfumes usually burst out with ripe, fresh edible fruity notes that feature the scent of fruits other than citrus, such as peach, black currant, mango, passion fruit, and others. With all of the different fruity perfumes out there, it can be hard to choose the right one that is going to work for you!

Minty

Mint is a type of fragrance note that is often described as fresh, cool, and invigorating. It can be used in a variety of fragrance types, including perfumes, colognes, and personal care products. The scent of mint is typically derived from the essential oils of mint plants such as peppermint, spearmint.

Pungent

Something that is pungent has a strong, sharp smell or taste which is often so strong that it is unpleasant.hot, peppery, piquant, sharp,sarcastic, mordant, cutting; acrimonious, bitter. 4 keen, sharp.

Sweet

There are three main types of sweet perfumes: fruity, floral, and gourmand. Fruity scents tend to be light and refreshing, with a natural sweetness that comes from the addition of fruit notes like citrus or berries.

Woody

As the name suggests, woody fragrances are characterised by notes derived from wood materials such trees, roots, resins, moss and some leaves and grasses that hold earthy qualities.
